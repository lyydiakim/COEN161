Open Graph

What is an Open Graph?

An open graph is typically used to give a preview of a website in a more engaging method than a hyperlink. The Open Graph Protocol was first started by Facebook and shows a rich preview of a website. This can include more interactive forms of media like audio and video by turning the webpage into a "rich object social graph". Open Graph tags are inserted in the head of the document using meta tags to further specify the type and content of the open graph. The following property tag is typically "og", abbreviated for open graph, which is then followed by the type of media. This could be a url, image, text, video, etc.

Example: 

                    
                        <meta property="og:type" content="video.movie"/>

Twitter wanted to create a competing graph concept, coming out with their own meta tags. However, instead of using the property tag "og", twitter uses a name tag "twitter". 

        
                        <meta name="twitter:card" content="summary_large_image"/ >

As another example, these are the open graph tags that are visible on the SCU website. 

                
                        <meta property="og:url" content="https://www.scu.edu">
                        <meta property="og:title" content="Santa Clara University: The Jesuit University in Silicon Valley">
                        <meta property="og:type" content="article">
                        <meta property="og:description" content="Headquartered in the most innovative place on earth, SCU is
                        a private Jesuit university in Santa Clara, California, in Silicon Valley. Consistently recognized 
                        as one of the top universities in the nation, Santa Clara offers bachelor's, master's, and doctoral 
                        degrees through its six colleges. ">
                        <meta property="og:image" content="https://www.scu.edu/media/public/buildings-rooms-and-spaces/lctcl-view.jpg">
                        <meta property="og:image:secure_url" content="https://www.scu.edu/media/public/buildings-rooms-and-spaces/lctcl-view.jpg">
                        <meta property="og:image:alt" content="Santa Clara University campus">
